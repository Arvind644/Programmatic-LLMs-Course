# Climate Change: Current Understanding and Impacts

## Overview
Climate change refers to long-term shifts in temperatures and weather patterns. While these shifts can be natural, since the 1800s, human activities have been the main driver of climate change, primarily due to burning fossil fuels like coal, oil, and gas.

## Key Causes
- **Greenhouse Gas Emissions**: Carbon dioxide and methane trap heat in the atmosphere
- **Deforestation**: Reduces the Earth's capacity to absorb CO2
- **Industrial Activities**: Manufacturing processes release various pollutants
- **Agricultural Practices**: Livestock and fertilizer use contribute to methane emissions

3. **Environmental Changes**
   - Melting glaciers and ice sheets
   - Rising sea levels
   - Ocean acidification
   - Shifts in ecosystems and biodiversity

## Mitigation Strategies
- Transition to renewable energy sources
- Energy efficiency improvements
- Sustainable transportation
- Carbon capture and storage
- Reforestation and conservation efforts

## Adaptation Measures
- Building climate-resilient infrastructure
- Developing early warning systems
- Implementing sustainable agricultural practices
- Protecting coastal communities
- Preserving natural ecosystems

## Global Response
- Paris Agreement (2015)
- United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change
- National climate action plans
- Corporate sustainability initiatives
- Community-led environmental projects

The challenge of climate change requires coordinated global action, innovative solutions, and sustained commitment from all sectors of society.
